Uamuzi Bora is made up of a diverse team of clinicians, developers and educators from Kenya, the UK and Norway.

Kenya Team

The Kenya team work with the Ministry of Health and partners to support EMR implementation, education and training.

John Haskew

Director

John is a medical doctor and founded Uamuzi Bora in 2009. He currently works as an Academic Clinical Fellow in Public Health at the University of Oxford and spends his time between the UK and Kenya.

Collins Otwane

Project Co-ordinator

Collins is a project co-ordinator for Uamuzi Bora and supports all technical implementation of the EMR in Kenya, including maintenance of the server, laptops and network.

Lauryn Amiga

Project Co-ordinator

Lauryn is a project co-ordinator based at Vihiga District Hospital. She oversees implementation and co-ordination of activities with Ministry of Health and manages clinical and health records staff.

Charity Olubero

Research Co-ordinator

Charity is a research co-ordinator responsible for implementation of the EMR in four peripheral HIV clinics.

Reginah Wambui

Research Co-ordinator

Reginah is a research co-ordinator based at Kakamega Provincial General Hospital. As well as overseeing implementation and co-ordination of activities within the hospital, Reginah is working with Clinicians to evaluate the use of clinical decision support.

Development Team

The development team split their time between Europe and field trips to Kenya, and are responsible for developing all aspects of the EMR system from hardware and infrastructure choices through to user interface design.

Kenrick Turner

Development Lead

Kenrick is a medical doctor based in the UK. He co-founded the project with John in 2009 and continues to work on it in a volunteer capacity. He mainly works on the user interface side of things, and telling John what isn't possible and then usually proving himself wrong.
